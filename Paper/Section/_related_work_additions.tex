\paragraph{Bribery and crypto-economic security (expanded).}
The economic study of buying consensus participation predates smart-contract bribery: Bonneau showed
that a \emph{rented}---rather than owned---majority has no long-term stake to protect and is therefore
cheap to mobilize~\cite{bonneau2016rent}, and Buterin's $P{+}\epsilon$ argument showed that a credible
conditional payment can make the adversary's preferred action dominant at near-zero realized
cost~\cite{buterin2015pepsilon}. Smart contracts then made such bribes
trustless~\cite{mccorry2018smart,winzer2019temporary}; Judmayer et al.\ systematize the resulting
\emph{algorithmic incentive-manipulation} attacks and demonstrate cheap, crowdfundable, cross-chain
bribery that funds a double-spend on a target chain~\cite{judmayer2021sok,judmayer2021paytowin}, while
out-of-band collusion contracts make defection the strict equilibrium for a sub-threshold rational
coalition and even sketch evading slashing by censoring the proof~\cite{zhang2023outofband}.
Game-theoretic treatments price bribery against PoS safety and characterize when slashing and dilution
make it unprofitable~\cite{karakostas2024bribing}; the most recent line implements trustless contracts
that buy votes, force exits, and auction beacon manipulation~\cite{sookitoth2025bribers,kelkar2025omerta,austgen2025liquefaction},
and modern variants coerce attesters through credible commitments~\cite{sarenche2024commitment} or
buy block delay for MEV without triggering slashing at all~\cite{yang2024bride}. Newman further shows
the equilibrium per-validator bribe can fall well below naive expected-loss bounds via low
pivotality and externalized cost~\cite{newman2023decentralization}. All of this establishes that
equivocation is buyable, trustless, and far cheaper than total stake; none of it models the
small-committee quorum structure, the cross-shard realization of the violation, or the way
accountability \emph{latency} prices the bribe.

\paragraph{Economic limits, slashable safety, and accountability latency.}
A separate line bounds attacks economically rather than by a Byzantine fraction. Budish's flow-vs-stock
argument~\cite{budish2018economic} and its permissionless-consensus generalization establish that an
attack is deterred only when its cost exceeds its profit, and---decisively for us---that under partial
synchrony above the $1/3$ threshold targeted punishment \emph{cannot} be enforced before the violator
liquidates, so accountability has an intrinsic latency-bounded limit~\cite{budish2024economic}. STAKESURE
makes the cost-of-corruption vs.\ profit-from-corruption gate explicit and caps attacker profit by gating
off-chain and bridge value release on a reversion window~\cite{deb2024stakesure}; CoBRA independently
bounds finalizable value below the slashable stake so a rational validator's gain cannot exceed its
bond~\cite{avarikioti2025cobra}; and Bitcoin-enhanced PoS makes the unbonding delay the operative
security variable, racing slashing against withdrawal and gating safety on external
finality~\cite{tas2023bitcoin}. These results supply the accountability substrate we build on:
identifiability of culprits after a fork~\cite{civit2021polygraph}, per-protocol forensic support that
fixes how many of the necessary equivocators are provably catchable~\cite{sheng2021forensics}, the
accountable-finalized prefix that underlies finality-gating~\cite{neu2022accountability}, and the
detection-and-recovery machinery whose latency we abstract as $\Tacc$~\cite{gong2025recover}.
\emph{Our contribution is orthogonal to all of these:} they study a monolithic or single-chain (or
restaking) setting in which the adversary owns stake or value is throttled, and they assume slashing is
effectively immediate or that the violator must control a (super)majority. We instead study a globally
\emph{sub-threshold} adversary who, after committee assignment, \emph{buys} the $F{+}1$ equivocators one
$N{=}3F{+}1$ committee makes necessary, prices that bribe by the probability $q$ that the fraud proof is
enforced before stake becomes unslashable, and realizes value across the shard/bridge boundary in
$\Tsettle$ before $\Tacc$. Finality-gating (Proposition~\ref{prop:immunity}) is our specialization of the
accountable-finalized-prefix idea to cross-shard acceptance.

\paragraph{Sharding-specific corruption (addition).}
Closest in setting are adaptive-corruption analyses of sharding: Divide \& Scale formalizes when a
sharded ledger can stay consistent and scalable against epoch-adaptive vs.\ fully adaptive
adversaries, requiring succinct state-update proofs at epoch boundaries~\cite{avarikioti2019divide}, and
Free2Shard defends a globally sub-threshold but locally concentrated adaptive adversary by letting honest
nodes re-allocate across shards~\cite{rana2022free2shard}. These treat the captured committee as a
\emph{corruption}-budget event and target liveness/throughput; we treat it as an \emph{economic
procurement} event and ask what the captor extracts before accountability resolves. Restaking economics
similarly bounds attacks by an over-collateralization condition~\cite{durvasula2025restaking}, but
without the accountability-latency race.

\paragraph{Corrupted-shard tolerance (addition).}
Most directly, Camael~\cite{liu2025camael} formalizes exactly our structural premise---a single shard
exceeding its local BFT threshold (up to $2/3$ corrupt) while global stake remains below $1/3$---and
builds a \emph{defense}: it detects concealed safety violations via snapshots, convicts the offenders,
and reconfigures the shard. Camael is complementary to our work rather than overlapping: it is a
detect-and-remediate construction and does not price the corruption, model the cross-shard
value-extraction race, or establish a finality-gating immunity result. Tellingly, Camael's own
snapshot-and-conviction pipeline is an instance of the accountability latency $\Tacc$ that our race
turns on: the faster that loop, the larger our $q$ and the smaller our window.

% (4b) Add to the "Economic limits, slashable safety, and accountability latency." paragraph (Part 2b):
\paragraph{Bribing against an accountability window (addition).}
Closest to our race in spirit is the economic-censorship game of Berger et
al.~\cite{berger2025censorship}, in which an attacker bribes block proposers to censor a defender's
challenge transactions and \emph{exhaust the challenge-period time budget}, with the attacker/defender
budget ratio deciding who wins. This is the same fundamental move---paying to outlast accountability---
that our $\Tsettle<\Tacc$ gate captures, and the analogous incentive failure where winning a fraud
proof is itself unprofitable is studied by Lee~\cite{lee2025hollow}. We differ in mechanism and setting:
their adversary censors a single defender's transaction in an L1$\rightarrow$L2 challenge window,
whereas ours buys the $F{+}1$ quorum-intersection equivocators of an $N{=}3F{+}1$ committee, prices that
bribe by the enforcement probability $q$ (Eq.~\eqref{eq:bribe-individual}), and races \emph{irreversible
cross-shard/bridge settlement} against detection-and-slashing rather than depleting a fixed challenge
clock. Remote-staking with optimal economic safety~\cite{dong2024remotestaking} supplies the dual
defensive primitive---a remote unbonding protocol that guarantees slashing \emph{before} stake is
withdrawn---which is precisely the ``slash before unslashable'' condition our $q$ measures, and
Aegis~\cite{baron2025aegis} similarly keeps stake ``correct until withdrawal'' with committee resets.
